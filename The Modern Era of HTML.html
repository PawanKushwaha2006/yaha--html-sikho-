🔍 What is a Watchman?
A Watchman (also called Security Guard, Chowkidar, or Darban) is a person employed to guard and patrol buildings, residential complexes, offices, banks, ATMs, and other properties. Their main job is to prevent theft, vandalism, and unauthorized entry. In India, watchmen are found in almost every apartment, society, shop, and company.
The role dates back to ancient times when kings had palace guards. Today it's a professional security job with training, uniforms, and sometimes weapons licenses for armed guards.
📋 Main Duties & Responsibilities
🚪 Gate Control & Access Management
Check ID of visitors, maintain entry/exit register, stop unauthorized persons, open/close gates
👁️ Patrolling & Surveillance
Regular rounds of premises, check CCTV cameras, look for suspicious activity, ensure all doors/windows locked
🚨 Emergency Response
Call police/fire brigade during emergencies, first aid, handle theft/fire incidents, sound alarms
📝 Record Keeping
Maintain logbook, visitor register, incident reports, shift handover notes
📦 Parcel & Delivery Management
Receive courier/parcels, inform residents, maintain delivery register
🚗 Parking Management
Guide vehicles, prevent wrong parking, manage parking slots, note vehicle numbers

✅ Requirements to Become a Watchman
Education
Minimum 8th or 10th pass. For armed guard or corporate jobs, 12th pass preferred. No degree needed.
Age Limit
18 to 55 years usually. For armed guard: 21 to 45 years. Physical fitness is more important than age.
Physical Fitness
Good health, no major disease. Height 5'5"+ preferred. Should be able to stand/walk for 8-12 hours.
Training
PSARA license required for security agencies. 20-day training: drill, weapon handling, first aid, fire safety.
Documents
Aadhaar, PAN, Police Verification, Medical Certificate, Address Proof, Bank Account.
Skills
Alert mind, honest, basic Hindi/English, writing ability for registers, mobile phone operation.
⏰ Shift Timings & Work Pattern
Common Shifts:
- Day Shift: 6 AM to 6 PM (12 hours)
- Night Shift: 6 PM to 6 AM (12 hours)
- 8-Hour Shift: 3 shifts in 24 hours (6-2, 2-10, 10-6)
- Weekly Off: 1 day per week (usually rotational)
Work Environment: Mostly outdoor at gate/cabin. Night duty common. Have to work in rain, summer, winter. Festivals and holidays also on duty. Reliever comes for breaks.

⚖️ Pros & Cons of Watchman Job
Pros:
- No high education needed - 8th/10th pass is enough
- Steady monthly income with overtime
- Free accommodation in many places
- Fixed duty hours, clear responsibilities
- Respect in society for protecting people
- Government jobs available (Railway, Bank guards)

Cons:
- Long standing hours - 8 to 12 hours
- Night shifts affect health
- Work in all weather - rain, heat, cold
- Low starting salary
- No holidays on festivals
- Risk during theft/fire incidents
